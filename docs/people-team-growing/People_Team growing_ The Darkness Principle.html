# title

The Darkness Principle

# summary

As the organizations are complex systems, it's understandable that teams have to take decisions together. This follows from the Darkness Principle.

# subject

Empower people/the agile organization

# definition

The Darkness Principle states that each agent in a system is ignorant of all behaviours of the system. If an agent 'knew'the entire system, the complexity of the whole system would have to residein that agent [Cilliers:1998:4-5].In consequence, each team member posseses only a partial and incomplete mental model of the entire project. That's the reason behind a common planning and decision making. Scrum and Extremme Programming are fully applying this model by requesting the whole team to be present during daily stand-ups and scrum planning meetings. Managers feel often that the lose control if the teams decide together and often without them. Any actions to control teams, don't work longterm and lead to team's demotivation.

# motivation

Increases self-organization of agile teams and organizations.

# benefit

Nobody on an organization or a team has a complete view of all things that are happening. If teams are let to solve their problems and to make decisions together and by themselves, a manager / an organization actually increases the control over the situation from a positive perspective. The Darkness Principle makes it transparent, that the manager has to delegate the micromanagement to the agile team.

# procedure

The Darkness Principle can be introduced as an open discussion within the teams, workshops with the management and stakeholders and also within the organization.Best practices have shown, that summarizing the Darkness Principle with some keywords written on a wall in the team spaces, makes it more transparent and easy to remember. It is also visually available to everyone in the organization and is a good method to bring people back to the core of agile process.
